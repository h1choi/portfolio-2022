Book Covers

Lord of the Flies explores the dark side of human nature. The themes of innate evil, savagery, and the loss of innocence takes prominence. (Spoiler) In the story the boys hunt down a mother pig in a very distressing manner. The moment the pig’s head is staked on a field, chaos erupts as the children lose sight of what is righteous and civil. This symbolized the evil that is very prominent in the later story (also having flies swarm around the head, “Lord of the Flies” – another name for Beelzebub, a devil). I have applied that symbol on the cover in scribbly agitated lines to create a sinister look and layered it on top of the human figure resembling a child to imply its possession.


A Thousand Splendid Suns, a novel by Khaled Hosseini, is a breathtaking story set against the volatile events of 1960-2000’s Afghanistan - from the Soviet invasion to the reign of the Taliban to post-Taliban rebuilding - that puts the violence, fear, hope, and faith of this country in intimate, human terms. It is a tale of two generations of characters –Mariam, an illegitimate child, and Laila, an orphan– brought jarringly together by the tragic sweep of war and after marrying the same man, Rasheed. The struggle to survive, raise a family, find happiness are inextricable from the history playing out around them.
